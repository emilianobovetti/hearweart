The Hear We Art Team

We are a group of artists, creatives and designers who work together to create workshops tailored to the needs of students with visual and hearing impairments.

The Hear We Art project leader is Olga Hendel. Nigel Bartram helped develop and finance the original Hear We Art concept and Pilot Programme in 2017. He continues to support the project out of a wish to help, directly, children in need, especially those who must overcome disabilities and require support to have any chance of living an independent and fulfilling life.

Project Leader

Olga Hendel

Olga graduated from Camberwell College of Arts in 2015 and since then has been working as a freelance artist in Rome. In 2012 she co-founded IdenticonArts, a LdT Community Interest Company, based in London, whose objective is to work with disadvantaged groups, from youngsters through  to the elderly, encouraging self-expression through art and craft making. Among the other workshops she has delivered are the Skills Show at the Birmingham Festival, Art for inclusion in Ukraine and Sp8 for Arts London. She also curated a project which produced QUIET! Magazine, participated in the Homegrown festival in Brixton and the AR.CO laboratory.

With the support and help of Nigel, in 2016, Olga visited Sri Lanka to look for underprivileged groups of children who could potentially benefit from Art, Handicraft and Design workshops and met the Principal of Senkadagala School. Discussions quickly led to the development of Hear We Art. In 2017 Olga, led the team (comprising herself, Alessia and Morgan – see below) which successfully piloted the first Hear We Art series of workshops.

Nigel Bartam

HearWeArt’s patron and co-founder is British, retired and lives with his wife, Caroline, in France. Most of his UK career was in Financial Services and was followed by a move to France in the late 1990’s and complete change of direction, founding a language school and becoming a part time university Associate Professor.

A less welcome change was the arrival of Multiple Sclerosis, but a decided sliver-lining came in the form of getting to know Olga Hendel. She had volunteered to illustrate stories for a book Nigel is editing called, ‘MS A funny thing (sometimes)’, the stories being funny things which have happened to ‘MSers’ only because of their MS. The objective is to bring much needed smiles to the faces of MSers, their loved ones, carers etc. raise money for MS charities and awareness of how lives are affected by the illness. Please have a look at msafunnything.com.

Nigel and Caroline have two children and through a small charity (the Lotus Foundation), have given money to help with the education of poor Sri Lankan children, especially providing basics foryoung ones and bursaries for university for the older kids. When Nigel became aware that Olga wished to help children in developing countries through Art, he suggested a project in Sri Lanka based on his Lotus Foundation experience and old university friend with business interests there. After some deliberation and planning, he and Caroline funded an investigatory visit in 2016 and the 2017 pilot programme.

Today, Nigel raises money for and underwrites HearWeArt, with Olga sets the objectives and priorities for the programmes and monitors the results and agrees budgets and expenditure.

2D Animation Tutor

Niroshan Bandara

Overcoming the serious disability of having no arms, which means Niroshan has to use his toes to operate a computer keyboard, he has developed a successful international career as a 2D and 3D graphic designer. After graduating from University of Moratuwa, in Sri Lanka, with a Bachelor of Design in Graphics & Visual Communication, Niroshan spent three years as an intern Motion Graphic Artist at MTV in the US where he developed his 2D / 3D Animation skills, learned to share ideas, and problem-solve in a high-pressured environment. Niroshan created many storyboards which formed the basis of US local and international TV advertisements as well as developing many graphic themes for MTV.

In the three years since he returned from the US, Niroshan has built up a business as a freelance artist, developing a portfolio of local Sri Lankan and international clients, He’s also now a visiting lecturer on for graphic web & interactive design and animation in several Sri Lankan universities and academies.

We are delighted to be working with Niroshan, not only for the graphic design skills and experience he brings, but also because it marks an important step in our aim of Hear We Art transitioning to being a programme which locals will be able to deliver autonomously. And perhaps most important of all, Niroshan will give the children a huge motivational boost, showing how it is perfectly possible to succeed despite having to overcome serious disabilities.

Graphic Design Tutor

Alessia Arcuri

​

Alessia is an Italian graphic designer, based in London. She graduated with a First-Class Honours degree in Graphic and Media Design from the London College of Communication (University of the Arts) in 2016. Alessia’s design practice encompasses both analogue and digital. She works in  a variety of fields, including online and offline editorial design, brand identity, web design and animation. She is currently Lead Designer and Art Director for Screen Shot Magazine, curating the design for both the online and offline versions of the editorial platform. Alessia has also worked for Tate Modern in London, designing the poster programme for Future Medina Workshop in late 2016. Other clients and collaborations includes: London Science Museum, Jungle Creations and Downstairs Collective.

Art Tutor and Mural Artists

Morganico

Morganico is a British artist who started from a graffiti background as a youth. He has been working in London since 1996, producing large scale public murals for the London Boroughs of Southwark, Lambeth and Hillingdon, plus a variety of other clients & personal street art projects. He is one of the artists who has been selected to join the prestigious Chelsea Art Club, which was set up Victorian times by James McNeil Whistler of the pre-Raphaelite fame. His most recent large-scale tree carving sculpture can be seen outside the Imperial War museum. Morganico also conducts spray paint workshops with school children for entertainment agencies.

Nicole White

Nicole is a Mural Artist and Storyteller based in Sheffield, England. She is interested in the link between creativity and education, and how to encourage engagement using playful ways of making. She has explored this through through leading workshops, community mural projects, mainstream and alternative school settings in England and Peru, creating art and murals with young people. Currently she is working as a freelance Mural Artist. She painted her first mural in Pisco, Peru for an Earthquake relief organisation called Pisco Sin Fronteras and has been passionate to share the empowerment of mural painting ever since.
